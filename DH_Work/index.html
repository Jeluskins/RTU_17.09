About the Project

Colombia's War for Independence occurred from 1810 to 1820 with the initial steps to unify against Spanish authority. The events of July 20, 1810 in Santa Fe de Bogota in the Spanish colonial Viceroyalty of New Granada was known as The Colombian Declaration of Independence. They resulted in the establishment of a Junta de Santa Fe that day. The experience in self-government eventually led to the creation of Gran Colombia (Dennis M. Hanratty & Sandra W. Meditz, 1988). Whenever a war took place, males would be the major role either due to their physical power or the conventionally social positions. However, women are never absent. There were many women being executed in Colombia's War for Independence and They are referred to as Colombia’s national heroes, like Policarpa Salavarrieta, who was known as " La Pola ''. She was captured by Spanish Royalists and ultimately executed for high treason. The Day of the Colombian Woman is commemorated on the anniversary of her death. She is now considered a heroine of the independence of Colombia. There are a number of women whose names are alive for Colombians and this is also what this project is going to focus on.

Plan

Data sorting.

After importing the dataset into Tableau, the tool has automatically set dedicated categories for us according to the existing columns in the file. While importing the dataset in csv. format to Recogita, we could manipulate data reordering based on each column and quickly changed its order based on our need when dealing with them.

Chart making.

Using Tableau, we have created 3 different charts respectively visualizing names, years and places in 3 working sheets. Different colours which were applied to different chunks of data have made a clearer demonstration and have helped us to distinguish facts of each woman easier.

Map creating.

e have created two maps in our projects - one based on the dataset and the other based on a merged text file in which the history of Colombia’s war has been described. For the dataset the map has been created faster, the places have been listed clearly in a column. However, the text where contents were obtained from several sources requires more effort, the text analyzing process has been involved here.

Analyzing and results.

Our analysis started from a brief view of our data - the simplest graphic displaying names of executed women in the dataset and the chart showing women who have the same names. The second part of our argument introduced the combination of the visualized dataset and the map generated from the external text we have collected. In this process, we have explained various remarkable data by putting them into the consideration of historical factors during the period of war. The last part focused on some absent data. Both in the second and third parts, we made a few assumptions according to the relations among data as well as between the dataset and the collected text.
